Who makes cars with higher fuel efficiency¶

In [41]:
sn.boxplot(data=auto_df, x= 'origin',y='mpg');

We are aware that Japanese make more fuel efficient cars than Americans do. Let us see if this is true.

In [26]:
sn.distplot(auto_df[auto_df.origin==1]['mpg'],label="American");
sn.distplot(auto_df[auto_df.origin==3]['mpg'],label="Japanese");
plt.legend();

We can see from the above chart that American car's fuel efficiency is somewhere between 10-15mpg whereas most of the Japanese cars are concentrated in the range 30-40mpg. But is this enough to conclude that Japanese cars are more fuel efficient than American cars? We always need to conduct statistical tests to make such a statement. We cannot conclude on the basis of visualisation alone

Two-sample t-test for hypothesis testing

In [28]:
stats.ttest_ind(auto_df[auto_df.origin==1]['mpg'],auto_df[auto_df.origin==3]['mpg'])
Out[28]:
Ttest_indResult(statistic=-12.67722648020723, pvalue=3.370227003938695e-30)

Based on the above t-test we can conclude at 0.05 significance level, that the null hypothesis is rejected.(p-value <0.05). Japanese cars are more fuel efficicent than American make cars.

We can see that majority of the American cars have higher Horsepower compared to Japanese make

Let us conduct a 2-sample t-test to see if the above hypothesis holds good.`

In [8]:
stats.ttest_ind(auto_df[auto_df.origin==1]['horsepower'],auto_df[auto_df.origin==3]['horsepower'])
Out[8]:
Ttest_indResult(statistic=8.460994146188728, pvalue=9.438939723740944e-16)

Based on the above t-test, we can conclude at the 0.05 significance level , that the null hypothesis is rejected(p-value<0.05).American make cars in the dataset, have a higher horsepower than Japanese cars
